Send `/wallet` to open the wallet panel. From there, three operations:

## Deposit

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap Deposit">
    Choose **SOL** or **USDC**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Get the address">
    The bot replies with your wallet address (copyable) and a QR code.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send funds">
    From any Solana-compatible source. Confirmation typically arrives in seconds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The bot will DM you when the deposit is detected on-chain (you can disable this in `/settings` notifications).

## Withdraw

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap Withdraw">
    Choose the asset (SOL or USDC).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick amount">
    Use a percentage preset (`25 / 50 / 100%` of balance) or type a custom amount.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter destination">
    Paste a Solana address. If you have a [withdrawal allowlist](/web-app/settings/security) and the amount exceeds the threshold, the bot only accepts allowlisted destinations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize">
    Above the security threshold, the bot prompts for 2FA / passkey. Below it, just an Authorize tap.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirmation">
    The bot returns the TX link once the network confirms.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Only send to Solana addresses. Other chains are not recoverable.
</Warning>

## Reset wallet

The reset flow lets you generate a fresh wallet for the active account. Used rarely — typically when you suspect a wallet has been associated with risky activity and you want a clean address.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap Reset">
    The bot warns you that any pending withdrawals will be cancelled.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Funds check">
    Reset is blocked if the wallet has a non-zero balance — withdraw or consolidate first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm twice">
    Reset is irreversible; the bot requires a second confirmation tap.
  </Step>
  <Step title="New wallet">
    A fresh Solana address is generated and shown.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The old wallet remains in the system as an archived record (you can still see its trade history), but it's no longer the active wallet for trading.

## Multi-wallet limits in the bot

The bot's `/wallet` command shows your **primary** wallet only. To see and manage all wallets in one view, use the [Portfolio web page](/web-app/portfolio/overview). The bot can switch the primary wallet (in `/settings`), but doesn't have a full list UI.
