The Znox bot can be added to any Telegram group or channel. Once it's in, trades from a wallet you've linked to that group post automatically as broadcasts with Quick Ape buttons — turning your group into a live trade feed.

## Who can do this

Only the **group owner** can add the Znox bot. Non-owner additions are rejected automatically — the bot leaves with a polite explanation message. This is anti-abuse: it prevents random members from spamming groups with bot integrations.

For channels, the same rule applies — only the channel creator can authorize.

## Add the bot

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your group/channel settings">
    Tap the group/channel name → **Add Members** → search `@znox_trading_betabot`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Grant admin permissions">
    The bot needs admin privileges with at minimum **Post Messages** permission. Without it, broadcasts can't be posted.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Bot greeting">
    Once admin, the bot posts a short welcome message confirming it's ready.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Link a wallet to the group

After the bot joins, it generates an **invite link** (or "broadcast link") for you to use to bind a wallet to the group:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap the welcome message">
    The greeting message has a "Set up broadcasting" button (or DM the bot if you missed it).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a wallet">
    The bot lists your wallets. Choose which one will broadcast its trades to this group.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable broadcasting">
    Confirm. From that moment, every trade from the linked wallet posts to the group as a [broadcast](/telegram-bot/groups/broadcasts).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Multi-group setup

A single wallet can broadcast to multiple groups — you can repeat the link flow with different groups, all pointing to the same wallet.

Conversely, different wallets can broadcast to different groups, so you can run separate trading channels for separate strategies without mixing them.

## Disable broadcasting

To stop broadcasting from a wallet to a group:

- In the bot DM, send `/settings` → choose the wallet → **Group broadcasts** → toggle off the group.
- Or: remove the bot from the group entirely (the bot's broadcast subscriptions to that group are deleted).

## Channel vs group

The setup is the same for both. Channels behave slightly differently in Telegram — only admins can post — but the broadcast UX is identical from the viewer's side.
