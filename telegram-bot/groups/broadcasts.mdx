Once a wallet is [linked to a group](/telegram-bot/groups/setup), every trade from that wallet posts as a **broadcast message** in the group. Group members can tap **Quick Ape** to copy the trade in one move.

For the underlying mechanic, see [Social-trading loop](/concepts/social-trading-loop).

## What a broadcast message looks like

The bot posts a message with:

- **Trader** identity (username + avatar)
- **Action** — `BOUGHT $5 of TOKEN @ $1.2M mcap` or `SOLD`
- **Token info** — symbol, price, market cap snapshot
- **Quick Ape button** — a deeplink that opens the bot DM in the trade flow
- **Open in Web** button — link to the [Terminal](/web-app/terminal/overview) for the same token

## What happens when a member taps Quick Ape

<Steps>
  <Step title="Bot DM opens">
    Telegram opens a private chat with the Znox bot, pre-loaded with the broadcast context (via a [slink](/concepts/slinks)).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Token card appears">
    The bot shows the same token card as a normal `/search`. The aper sees current price, security info, etc.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Buy presets">
    The aper picks an amount (their own preset, not the broadcaster's). Authorizes the trade.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attribution recorded">
    The trade is tagged with the originating broadcast ID. The broadcaster earns 25% of fees as a [Signal reward](/web-app/rewards/signals).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Open / close / increase / decrease

For most tokens, every trade posts a broadcast. **SOL is special** — the system recognizes that SOL is the base currency and tracks discrete buy/sell cycles:

- **OPEN broadcasts** — first buy of a SOL cycle.
- **CLOSE broadcasts** — when sold ≥ bought (cycle ends).
- **Increase/decrease broadcasts on SOL are skipped.** Only meaningful state transitions get posted.

This avoids spamming groups with every micro-trade on SOL.

## Reactions in groups

In Telegram, group members can use the bot's broadcast message reactions (the same 🔥🚀💰💀👍❤️😂😯 set as the web feed). Reactions are aggregated across web and Telegram — a 🔥 in the group counts the same as a 🔥 on the web feed.

## Muting

Group admins can:

- Pin the bot's broadcasts to keep them visible.
- Mute non-broadcast bot messages while keeping broadcasts (Telegram's per-bot mute settings).
- Per-user: members who don't want broadcast pings can disable bot notifications for the group on their device.
