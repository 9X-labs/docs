**Track Wallets** lets you add any Solana wallet address and stream its trades live. Useful for following KOLs whose calls aren't on Znox, monitoring competitors, or just keeping an eye on a friend.

This is different from **Following** a Znox broadcaster — Track Wallets works for *any* on-chain wallet, whether or not they use Znox.

## Adding a wallet

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enter the address">
    Paste a Solana wallet public key into the **Track Wallets** input.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optionally label it">
    Add a name so you can recognize it in the list (e.g., "Murad", "Frank degen").
  </Step>
  <Step title="It appears in your tracking list">
    The wallet's recent trades populate immediately, and new trades stream in live.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What you see per tracked wallet

Each trade appears as a row with:

- **Side** (BUY / SELL)
- **Token** — symbol + logo
- **Amount** — in token units and quote-token equivalent
- **Price** at execution
- **Timestamp**
- **TX hash** (linkable to a Solana explorer)

## Live updates

Trades are pushed via WebSocket as soon as the parser indexes the transaction. Latency from on-chain to your screen is typically 1–3 seconds. See [Real-time architecture](/concepts/real-time-architecture).

## Acting on a tracked trade

Click any trade row to open the [Terminal](/web-app/terminal/overview) for that token. From there you can decide whether to follow the trade with your own.

<Tip>
  Track Wallets is read-only by design — no auto-copy. Solana speed makes "see → decide → trade" feasible without giving the platform permission to act on your behalf.
</Tip>

## Removing a wallet

Each row in the tracked list has a remove button. Removing it stops the live stream; the trade history view keeps a snapshot until refresh.
