**Track Socials** complements [Track Wallets](/web-app/traders-hub/track-wallets). Where Track Wallets surfaces what people are *trading*, Track Socials surfaces what they're *talking about*.

## Adding an account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pick a platform">
    X (Twitter), Telegram, or Discord.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the handle">
    `@username` for X / Telegram, `username#1234` or invite for Discord.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optionally label it">
    Same as Track Wallets — give it a name.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What you see

A live feed of posts from your tracked accounts, with token mentions highlighted. Each post shows:

- Author + platform icon
- Post text
- Tokens mentioned (as clickable badges → Terminal)
- Post timestamp
- Link out to the original post

## Why track social separately from wallets

A KOL might tweet about a token before they buy it (or never buy it themselves). Track Socials catches the conversation; Track Wallets catches the action. Together they give you both signal types.

## Right-side panel

The Traders Hub track view has a right-side toggle to switch between:

- **Tracked socials** — the feed described above.
- **X live feed** — recent posts globally that match any tokens you've trade-history with, even if their authors aren't tracked.

## Limitations

- Telegram group/channel tracking requires the channel to be public or the bot to be a member.
- X tracking depends on API rate limits — high-volume accounts may have a small (~30s) delay vs. real-time.
- Discord tracking is currently limited to public servers the Znox bot has been invited to.
