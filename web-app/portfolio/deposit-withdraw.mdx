Funds flow in and out of Znox wallets via real Solana transactions. Deposits are instant once the network confirms; withdrawals above a threshold pass through a security gate.

## Deposit

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Deposit">
    On the [Portfolio](/web-app/portfolio/overview) page, click **Deposit**. A modal opens with the destination wallet's address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the address or scan the QR">
    Send SOL or USDC from any Solana-compatible source (exchange, another wallet, friend).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for confirmation">
    Solana confirms in seconds. The Portfolio view reconciles within ~10s of the on-chain confirm.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Only send Solana network assets. Tokens from other chains (Ethereum USDC, BSC, etc.) are not recoverable.
</Warning>

## Withdraw

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Withdraw">
    Open the Withdraw modal. Pick the asset (SOL or USDC) and the amount.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter destination">
    Paste a Solana address. If you have a [withdrawal allowlist](/web-app/settings/security) and the amount exceeds the threshold, only allowlisted addresses are accepted.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pass the security gate (if required)">
    Above the threshold, you'll be prompted for: 2FA code, passkey signature, or both, depending on your security settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    The withdrawal transaction signs and submits. On-chain confirmation typically arrives in under 2 seconds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The withdrawal threshold

By default, small withdrawals go through without a security gate — convenience for routine operations. Larger withdrawals trigger the gate. You can:

- **Lower the threshold** (more friction, more safety).
- **Add an allowlist** — restricts large withdrawals to pre-approved addresses regardless of session state.

Both knobs are in [Settings → Security](/web-app/settings/security).

<Tip>
  An allowlist + a low threshold is the strongest practical configuration. An attacker who fully takes over your session still can't withdraw to a new address.
</Tip>

## Network fees

Both deposits and withdrawals pay a small Solana network fee (in SOL, paid by the wallet). Withdraw amounts are reduced by the network fee — you get out slightly less than you input.
