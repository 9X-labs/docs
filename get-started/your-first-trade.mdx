You have a wallet, you have SOL. Time to trade. Here's the shortest path to your first buy on each surface.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Web App">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Search a token">
        Hit `/` to focus the search bar in the top nav. Type a symbol or paste a Solana token address. Click the result.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Land in the Terminal">
        You're now on the per-token page. The chart is on the left, the order entry panel is on the right.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Pick an amount">
        In the **Buy** tab, click a preset amount (`0.1`, `0.5`, `1` SOL) or type a custom amount. The estimated token output appears below.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Check slippage and MEV">
        Default slippage is fine for most trades. **MEV protection** is on by default with the *good* Jito tier — leave it unless you know what you're doing. See [MEV & Jito](/concepts/mev-and-jito).
      </Step>
      <Step title="Hit BUY">
        Confirm the dialog. The trade is signed and submitted to Solana. You'll see a status update within seconds.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Verify">
        Your new position shows up in the **Holdings** section of the same page, and in **Portfolio → Holdings**.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Telegram Bot">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Send the token">
        DM the bot ([@znox_trading_betabot](https://t.me/znox_trading_betabot)) with a token address or symbol. Or send `/search`.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Read the token card">
        The bot replies with a card: price, market cap, liquidity, holder count, social links, security badges.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Pick an amount">
        Tap a preset Buy button (`0.1`, `0.5`, `1` SOL) or use the custom input.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Confirm">
        Tap **Authorize**. The bot updates the message with the trade status. Confirmation usually arrives in a few seconds.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Check positions">
        Send `/positions` to see your open trades, including the one you just made.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Selling works the same way

Switch the Buy tab to **Sell** (web) or use the sell preset buttons on a token card (bot). You sell a percentage of your position — common presets are 25%, 50%, 100%.

<Tip>
  For SOL specifically, "sell 100%" leaves a tiny residual to keep the wallet usable for fees. This is intentional.
</Tip>

## What happens behind the scenes

When you hit Buy, Znox:

1. Picks the best DEX route across Solana liquidity (see [DEX routing](/concepts/dex-routing)).
2. Builds a swap transaction with your slippage tolerance.
3. If MEV protection is on, bundles it through Jito with your tip tier (see [MEV & Jito](/concepts/mev-and-jito)).
4. Signs the transaction with your wallet's key (held in the Znox key-management system).
5. Submits it and waits for finality.

The whole loop is typically sub-second.

## If something goes wrong

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Slippage exceeded">
    Price moved more than your tolerance between quote and execution. Increase slippage in **Terminal Settings** or retry — common on volatile tokens.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Insufficient SOL for fees">
    Your wallet doesn't have enough SOL to cover the network fee + priority fee + Jito tip. Top up.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Transaction not confirmed">
    Solana didn't include the tx in a block. Usually Jito reroutes; if not, the trade auto-cancels and your funds stay where they were.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
