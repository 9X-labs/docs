When a Quick Ape button in a Telegram group leads someone to a token, two things have to be true:

1. The buy needs to **execute against the same token** the broadcast referenced.
2. The Quick Ape needs to **credit the original broadcaster** so they earn their 25%.

Both rely on a small piece of infrastructure called a **slink** — a *broadcast deeplink* that carries the context from the original signal all the way to the executed trade.

## What a slink is

A slink is a short opaque token (`?slink=KEY`) attached to the Quick Ape button. It points to a cached record on the Znox backend that holds:

- The originating **broadcast ID**
- The **token mint** the broadcast referenced
- The **user ID** of the broadcaster (so rewards route correctly)
- Optionally, the **Telegram group ID** the signal was posted to (for group-level analytics)

The slink itself is meaningless — it's just a key. The cached record behind it is what makes the system work.

## Lifetime

Slinks live in cache for **1 hour** from the moment the broadcast is posted. After that:

- The Quick Ape button still works.
- The token resolves correctly (the URL itself usually carries the mint as a fallback).
- But the broadcast attribution is dropped — the trade executes as a normal buy, no rewards routed.

In practice, broadcasts get aped within seconds to minutes. The 1h window covers ~99% of activity.

## Why this design

Three constraints made slinks the right primitive:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Telegram URL length" icon="ruler">
    Telegram inline buttons have URL length limits. We can't pack the full broadcast context into the link itself — a short opaque key is needed.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Privacy" icon="eye-slash">
    Broadcaster identity and group ID shouldn't be readable from the URL. A cache lookup keeps that data server-side.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Token swap protection" icon="shield">
    Without slinks, an attacker could rewrite the URL to point at a different token while keeping the broadcast attribution. Slinks bind broadcast → token at the source.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Lifecycle" icon="hourglass">
    Some broadcasts get aped instantly, some not at all. A short-lived cache is cheaper than persisting every broadcast context forever.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What you'll see in practice

You don't interact with slinks directly. They show up only in two places:

- **Telegram Quick Ape button URL** — `https://t.me/znox_trading_betabot?start=ape_<KEY>` (the `KEY` is the slink).
- **Web app Quick Ape URL** — same pattern via `znox.io` direct.

If you tap a Quick Ape that's older than an hour, the bot still walks you into the trade flow with the right token, but the broadcaster won't earn the signal share on it.
