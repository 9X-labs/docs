Most trading apps stop at "you trade." Znox adds a second loop on top: **what you trade can be broadcast to the world, and when others copy it, you earn**.

The whole flow is built around a primitive called a **broadcast**.

## The four-step loop

<Steps>
  <Step title="You trade">
    You buy or sell a token from any wallet that has broadcasting enabled.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Znox broadcasts">
    A signal card is created in the **Traders Hub → Broadcast** feed and (optionally) posted to any Telegram groups you've linked to that wallet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Others Quick Ape">
    Anyone seeing the broadcast can hit **Quick Ape** to place the same buy with one click. They choose their own size.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You earn">
    Every Quick Ape pays a fee. **25% of that fee goes to you** — the broadcaster — for as long as the apers keep trading the position you opened.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What a broadcast looks like

A broadcast card carries:

- **Trader identity** — username, avatar, optional X badge
- **What was traded** — `BOUGHT $5 of SOL @ $49.68B mcap`
- **Token chart** — price action since the broadcast, with your entry marked
- **Reactions** — 8 emoji buttons (🔥🚀💰💀👍❤️😂😯) any viewer can tap
- **Comments** — threaded discussion
- **Quick Ape button** — one-click buy at the viewer's preset amount
- **Ape count** — how many people have already aped it

## The reward math

| Source | Share |
| --- | --- |
| **Referral** (someone you invited trades) | 50% of their fees |
| **Signal** (someone Quick Apes your broadcast) | 25% of their fees |
| **Both** (your referral Quick Apes your broadcast) | 25% referral + 25% signal = 50% combined |

Earnings accrue forever — there is no cap, no expiry. As long as the person keeps trading the position they aped, you keep earning.

The breakdown chart on the **Rewards → Referral** page visualizes this — orange line is referral, purple is signal, green dashed is the combined ref+signal case.

## Turning broadcasts on

Each wallet has its own broadcast settings — you choose which wallets broadcast and which stay silent. The default for new wallets is **off**. Enable it in **Portfolio → wallet → Broadcast settings**.

## Why this exists

Most copy-trading platforms create a two-class system: a small group of "verified providers" who get paid, everyone else who pays. Znox's loop is symmetric — anyone can broadcast, anyone can Quick Ape, and anyone can earn from either side. The only filter is whether your trades are good enough that others want to copy them.
