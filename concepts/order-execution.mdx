A trade on Solana is a signed transaction that your wallet submits to the network and validators include in a block. Three knobs control how it executes: **slippage**, **priority fee**, and **order type**.

## Slippage

Slippage is the maximum price difference (in percent) you'll accept between when the quote was generated and when the trade actually executes. Solana blocks confirm in ~400ms, but on volatile tokens that's enough for the price to move.

| Slippage | Use when |
| --- | --- |
| **0.5%** (default) | Liquid tokens, tight spreads |
| **1–2%** | Trending tokens, normal volatility |
| **5%+** | Memecoins, post-launch tokens, fast-moving markets |

If the actual fill would exceed your slippage, the trade reverts on-chain. **Funds aren't lost — the transaction simply doesn't happen.** You'll see a slippage error and can retry with a wider tolerance.

<Warning>
  Setting slippage to 50%+ effectively disables the safety net. Use only when you understand the risk — bots can sandwich very loose trades.
</Warning>

## Priority fee

Solana validators order transactions in a block by **compute-unit price** (priority fee). On a quiet network, you don't need any. On a busy network, transactions without priority fees can wait minutes or never confirm.

Znox sets a sensible default priority fee per trade. You can override it per-trade in the Terminal settings or globally in **Terminal Settings → Defaults**.

## Market vs limit orders

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Market" icon="bolt">
    Executes at the best available price now. Subject to slippage tolerance. This is the default and what 95% of trades use.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Limit" icon="hourglass">
    Executes only when the token reaches a target price. Lives as an open order in **Portfolio → Orders** until filled or cancelled.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What a trade actually does

<Steps>
  <Step title="Quote">
    Znox queries DEX pools and computes the best route + expected output (see [DEX routing](/concepts/dex-routing)).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build">
    A swap instruction is constructed against the chosen venue's program with your slippage tolerance encoded.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Bundle (optional)">
    If MEV protection is on, the transaction is bundled with a Jito tip (see [MEV & Jito](/concepts/mev-and-jito)).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign">
    The wallet's key (held in Znox key management) signs the transaction.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Submitted to Solana via the chosen path. Confirmation typically arrives in under 2 seconds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Failure modes you'll see

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Slippage exceeded">
    Price moved beyond your tolerance. Increase slippage, or wait for the token to settle.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Insufficient SOL for fees">
    The wallet doesn't have enough SOL to cover network fee + priority fee + Jito tip. Top up.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Transaction not confirmed in time">
    The network was congested and your priority fee wasn't competitive. Increase priority fee or wait.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Pool froze or migrated">
    Rare — the underlying pool changed state mid-trade. Retry; the router will pick a different venue.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
