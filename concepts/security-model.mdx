Znox holds wallet keys in a managed key system so you don't have to. That convenience comes with the responsibility of making sure no one else can access your account. The security model is a defense-in-depth stack — each layer is independent, and the most sensitive operations require multiple layers to pass.

## Authentication layers

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Sign-in provider" icon="key">
    Google or Telegram OAuth. The first line of defense — anyone who can log into your Google or Telegram can log into Znox unless you add more layers.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Passkey" icon="fingerprint">
    Optional WebAuthn credential bound to your device's biometric (FaceID, TouchID, Windows Hello). Phishing-resistant — passkeys can't be replayed against fake sites.
  </Card>
  <Card title="2FA (TOTP)" icon="mobile-screen">
    Time-based one-time passwords from an authenticator app. Adds a second factor on top of OAuth.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Session management" icon="list-check">
    See every active session (browser, device, IP). Revoke individual sessions or "log out everywhere" on demand.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Withdrawal allowlist

By default, you can withdraw to any Solana address. Once you add an **allowlist**, large withdrawals (above a threshold) can only go to allowlisted addresses.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add an address">
    **Settings → Security → Withdrawal allowlist → Add**. Paste the destination Solana address and label it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pass the security challenge">
    Adding to the allowlist itself triggers a multi-phase challenge — passkey + 2FA + email confirmation. This prevents an attacker who briefly took over the session from instantly adding their own address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for the gate">
    New allowlist entries are subject to a short hold before they become valid for withdrawals. This gives you a window to revoke if something looks wrong.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  An allowlist is the single most effective protection against account compromise. Even if an attacker fully takes over your session, they can't withdraw to an address that wasn't pre-approved.
</Tip>

## Multi-phase recovery

Recovering account access (e.g., you lost your phone with the 2FA app) goes through a deliberate multi-step flow:

1. **Initiate** — request a recovery from the login screen.
2. **Challenge** — Znox issues a challenge that asks for evidence you're the account owner (originating provider sign-in, recovery email confirmation).
3. **Wait period** — once verified, a cooling-off period applies before the new credentials become live. Active sessions are notified during this period.
4. **Finalize** — at the end of the wait, the new credentials replace the old ones.

The wait period exists specifically so that *if* an attacker were the one initiating recovery, the real owner has time to abort it.

## Passphrase export

Znox holds your wallet keys, but you can export an encrypted passphrase that lets you derive the keys yourself. This is your escape hatch — even if Znox went away, your funds are recoverable.

Export is gated behind:
- 2FA (if enabled)
- Email confirmation
- A separate session reauth

Treat the exported passphrase like you would a hardware wallet seed: never share, never paste into any input that didn't generate it.

## What we don't do

- We don't email or DM you asking for your passphrase, 2FA code, or session cookie. Anyone who does is impersonating us.
- We don't auto-link wallets you didn't explicitly add. If you see a wallet you don't recognize, treat it as a security incident and revoke sessions.
- We don't whitelist your withdrawal address automatically. Every allowlist entry requires the full challenge.
