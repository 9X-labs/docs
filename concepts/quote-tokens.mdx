When you trade `TOKEN_X`, you trade *something else* against it. That something else is the **quote token**. On Znox, the quote token is either **SOL** or **USDC**.

## How the quote token affects what you see

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Prices" icon="dollar-sign">
    A token's price is shown in your selected quote: e.g., `0.000123 SOL` or `$0.0273`.
  </Card>
  <Card title="P&L" icon="chart-line">
    Realized and unrealized P&L are tracked in the quote token your position was opened in.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Preset amounts" icon="grid">
    Buy/sell presets are denominated in your quote: `0.1 / 0.5 / 1 SOL` vs `10 / 50 / 200 USDC`.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Order entry" icon="square-check">
    The "amount" field accepts the quote token unless you switch to entering token units.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## When SOL is the right choice

- **You hold mostly SOL.** Trading SOL→token is one swap; trading USDC→token might involve an extra hop.
- **You want fast execution.** Most Solana liquidity is in SOL pairs, so SOL routes are typically deeper and tighter.
- **You're following the broadcast feed.** Quick Ape always quotes in SOL.

## When USDC is the right choice

- **You're sizing in dollars.** "How many USDC am I risking" is easier to reason about than fluctuating SOL value.
- **You want stable P&L.** A token can pump in SOL terms and dump in USDC terms simultaneously if SOL itself moves. USDC removes that ambiguity.
- **You hold mostly stablecoins.** No reason to convert.

## SOL is special

SOL is the network's gas token *and* a tradable asset. Two consequences:

1. **Network fees.** Every Solana transaction pays a tiny fee in SOL — even when you're paying for a USDC-quoted trade. You always need a little SOL in the wallet.
2. **Selling 100% SOL leaves a residual.** A wallet with literally zero SOL can't sign anything, including a deposit-receive ack. We leave a small dust amount intentionally.

## Switching the default

Set your preferred quote in **Settings → Display** on the web app, or in the bot's settings menu. The setting syncs across both surfaces — change it on the bot, the web app picks it up.
