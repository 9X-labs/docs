When you submit a normal Solana transaction, anyone watching the mempool can see it before it lands in a block. **Sandwich bots** exploit this: they buy in front of your trade, let your buy push the price up, then sell into you for a profit. The cost is yours.

**MEV protection** on Znox routes your trades through [Jito](https://jito.network/) bundles instead of the public mempool, so they're invisible to bots until they execute. The trade-off is a small tip you pay to Jito for inclusion.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Bundle creation">
    Your swap transaction is wrapped in a Jito bundle along with a tip transaction.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Private submission">
    The bundle goes to Jito relayers, never the public mempool.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Atomic inclusion">
    A Jito-validated leader either includes the *whole* bundle in a block or none of it. No reordering, no insertion.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You get filled at quote price">
    No bot can squeeze in front. Slippage is just from natural pool depth, not from adversarial trades.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tip tiers

The tip you pay is what gets you inclusion. Znox offers four tiers, each backed by live tip-floor data refreshed every few seconds:

| Tier | What it pays | Use when |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Good** | 25th percentile of recent tips | Calm markets, small trades |
| **Fast** | 50th percentile | Normal volatility |
| **Turbo** | 75th percentile | Hot tokens, fast-moving markets |
| **Custom** | You enter the lamports | You know exactly what you want to pay |

<Note>
  Tip values change minute-to-minute as the network shifts. Znox shows the *current* tip for each tier so you're not guessing.
</Note>

## When to turn MEV protection off

Almost never. The tip is small (typically under $0.01) compared to what a sandwich bot would extract. The case for off:

- You're trading a token with no liquid market for sandwiching (e.g., a fresh PumpFun launch where you're a top-3 holder).
- You're the only one trading the pool at that moment.

Even then, the cost of leaving it on is negligible.

## Where to set it

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Per trade (Web)">
    In the Terminal order entry panel, the **MEV protection** toggle and **Jito tier** dropdown are visible right next to slippage. Changes apply to that trade only.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Default (Web)">
    **Terminal Settings** (gear icon) → set the default MEV protection and tier. Applies to every trade unless overridden.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Bot">
    `/settings` → choose Buy or Sell side → MEV Protection / Jito Tier. Settings sync with the web app.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
