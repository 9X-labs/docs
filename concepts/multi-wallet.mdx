Most trading apps tie your account to a single wallet. Znox doesn't. Your account is a container; your wallets are the on-chain identities you trade with.

## What a wallet is on Znox

A **wallet** on Znox is a real Solana account — it has a public key, holds SOL and SPL tokens, and signs transactions. It is held in our key-management system so you don't have to manage seed phrases, but the funds are yours and the wallet is fully on-chain.

## The primary wallet

Exactly one wallet at any time is your **primary**. It's what trades execute on by default and what shows first in **Portfolio**. You can change which wallet is primary at any time.

## Funds movement between wallets

Two operations let you move SOL/USDC between your own wallets without touching an external service:

- **Distribute** — split funds from one wallet across several. Useful for spinning up sub-wallets from a main funded wallet.
- **Consolidate** — pull funds from many wallets into the primary. Useful when you want to make a large trade or withdraw.

Both operations sign on-chain transactions; they're real Solana transfers, not internal accounting moves.

## Archive vs delete

You can **archive** a wallet to hide it from the main list without losing it. Archived wallets still hold any funds they had, and you can unarchive them at any time.

Wallets cannot be deleted — once a key exists, it exists. Withdrawing the balance to zero and archiving is the closest you get to "removing" a wallet.

## Where wallets live across surfaces

| Surface | Where wallets show |
| --- | --- |
| Web Portfolio | Full list with P&L per wallet, primary toggle, action buttons |
| Telegram `/wallet` | Summary of the primary wallet only |
| Telegram `/portfolio` | Aggregate across all wallets |
| Trade execution | Always the primary unless you switch in the Terminal |
