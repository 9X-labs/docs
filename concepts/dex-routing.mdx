Solana liquidity isn't in one place. The same token might trade on Pump.Fun, Raydium, Orca, Meteora, and a half-dozen other DEXs simultaneously, with different prices and depths. Picking the wrong one means worse fills.

Znox routes every trade through a **best-market selection** layer that compares quotes across supported venues and picks the one that gives you the best execution.

## How routing works

<Steps>
  <Step title="You hit Buy or Sell">
    Your trade size + token + quote token are passed to the routing engine.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Quote the venues">
    The engine pulls live pool state from every supported DEX and computes the expected output for your trade size on each.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick the winner">
    The venue with the best post-slippage output wins. For very large trades, multi-hop routes (e.g., split across two DEXs) are considered.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build and submit">
    The transaction is built against the chosen venue's program and submitted to Solana.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Supported DEXs

- **Pump.Fun** — pre-graduation memecoin trading
- **Raydium** — including AMM, CLMM, and CPMM pools
- **Orca** — including Whirlpools (concentrated liquidity)
- **Meteora** — DLMM and DAMM pools
- **PumpSwap** — post-graduation Pump.Fun routes
- **Jupiter** — fallback aggregator for routes Znox doesn't directly support

The list grows over time. Routing is transparent — you don't need to know which venue your trade hit.

## Why the price you see may differ slightly from the price you got

Two reasons:

1. **Display price is a midpoint.** The quote shown on the chart is between bid and ask. Your actual fill comes from one side of the book.
2. **Depth eats the spread.** A bigger trade walks down (or up) the order book / curve and gets a worse average price. This is reflected in the **estimated output** field below the amount input — that number is the price after walking the depth.

The slippage tolerance is your safety margin against this. If the post-walk price exceeds your tolerance, the trade reverts.

## Pool selection for memecoins

Memecoins often graduate from Pump.Fun to PumpSwap (and eventually Raydium). During and right after migration, liquidity is fragmented. Znox's router chooses the deepest pool at the moment of execution — but **expect wider spreads on freshly-graduated tokens**.
